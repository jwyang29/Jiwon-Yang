Overview

Light × Sound: A Responsive Architecture

Chorus is an interactive installation that treats sound and light as a single, unified material. A column of vertical tubes responds to ambient audio — each pipe independently lit, its brightness and color shifting as the collective soundscape in the room evolves.

The installation transforms passive listening into a visible, spatial phenomenon. When voices overlap, the light overlaps. When silence falls, the light breathes.

Technical Approach

Frequency as Form

Audio is analyzed in real time using FFT in Max/MSP. The frequency spectrum is divided into four bands — sub-bass, bass, mid, high — each mapped to one of the four pipe columns. Amplitude drives brightness; sustained tones create a slow wash of color that peaks and decays with the room's energy.

The diffusion material on each tube was selected after testing twelve different densities to find the balance between color fidelity and softness.

Context

Designed for Social Space

Chorus was exhibited in a gallery foyer — a transitional space where people pause briefly before entering an exhibition. The work is designed to reward attention without demanding it: it functions as ambient environment or, for those who notice, as an invitation to listen differently.
