Touch × Sand: A Physical Interface

Playground explores the boundary between physical material and digital feedback. A sand-based surface embeds capacitive sensors that translate pressure and gesture into real-time visual response — inviting a form of play that is simultaneously tactile, intuitive, and unexpected.

[ .Process ]

From Material to Interaction

The project began with material research — testing how different granular substances (sand, salt, rice) respond to touch in terms of both tactile feel and sensor conductivity. A 16×16 capacitive sensing array was selected for resolution and latency balance.

Pressure data streams via serial to TouchDesigner, where a particle system responds in real time: more pressure summons denser, brighter particles; a gentle trace leaves fading trails.

[ .Context ]

What the Hands Know

The most surprising outcome was observing how visitors — unprompted — began to draw letters, then erase them, then simply drag their fingers in slow circles. The interface invited a kind of meditative attention that touchscreens rarely afford.
